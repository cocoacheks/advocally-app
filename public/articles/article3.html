The Increasing Danger of Wildfires in Canada and How You Can Help Today

March 10, 2025

by Sania Baig

Blake, M. (Photographer). (2025, January 10). Fire in northeastern British Columbia [Photograph]. Reuters. https://www.barrons.com/articles/wildfire-risk-is-high-4-stocks-winners-6be19992

Canada is home to an abundance of lush forests, various wildlife, and unique natural habitats. Unfortunately, the more recent years have brought to us the increasing threat of wildfires. Wildfires have left a permanent impact on both natural ecosystems and Canadian communities nationwide and are ever-growing in frequency, both by human activity and climate change. In 2023, over 10 million hectares of forest were burned due to wildfires across Canada, marking one of the worst wildfire seasons in recorded history (Natural Resources Canada, 2023). Forced evacuations and health risks are some of the many impacts of these disasters.

Wildfires: What are they?

Wildfires are fast-growing fires that can increase rapidly across forests. Alberta, British Columbia, and Ontario are the most susceptible to the threat, with summer months being the most high in frequency. The 2023 wildfire season alone saw more than 1,000 active fires across the country (Environment Canada, 2023). These fires have destroyed entire landscapes, communities, and habitats.

Climate change (with the impact of warmer temperatures and droughts) in recent years has increased the amount of wildfires. In fact, Canada has seen an increase in the frequency of wildfires by nearly 50% over the past 30 years (Canadian Forest Service, 2022). This then contributes to global warming by releasing large amounts of carbon into the atmosphere. The wildfires in 2023 were responsible for emitting an estimated 100 million metric tonnes of carbon dioxide (Canadian Climate Institute, 2023).

How You Can Help Today

Canadian communities are working hard to mitigate the risks of the fires by investing in preventative measures and implementing improved measures to manage the fires. In 2022, the Canadian government allocated $100 million toward wildfire risk reduction initiatives (Public Safety Canada, 2022). The government and various organizations are taking action to better prepare for and respond to any upcoming risks.
