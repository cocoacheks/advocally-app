Understanding the Growing Crisis of Homelessness in Canada

March 12, 2025

by Sania Baig

Burston, C. (Photographer). (2020, March 11). A homeless person sleeps on the sidewalk in downtown Toronto [Photograph]. CBC News. https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/toronto/homeless-coronavirus-canada-toronto-covid19-1.5492322

According to the Canadian Alliance to End Homelessness (CAEH), up to 300,000 Canadians experience homelessness each year. From individuals sleeping on the streets to inadequate housing for families, itâ€™s much more complex than you think. The stats are even more alarming during the Canadian winter months, when countless people are turned away from shelters due to capacity limitations to fend for themselves in freezing temperatures.

There are a range of causes of homelessness. To name a few, high costs of housing, mental health struggles, lack of healthcare and support services, and inequality are some of the most common roots of the ever-growing crisis. Fluctuations in rent prices, especially in major Canadian cities such as Toronto, have made it difficult for large amounts of low-income individuals to find a stable place to live. According to the Universal Declaration of Human Rights and the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights; the right to adequate housing is recognized as a fundamental human right - yet there is little being done to mitigate homelessness.

To tackle homelessness in Canada, requires country wide support. Increasing affordable housing solutions and investing in support services around mental health or substance abuse can make a huge impact. Support and affordability should not be limited for individuals regardless of their background or circumstances.

You can be part of the bigger change, regardless of your own circumstances. Volunteering, donating to local charities, advocating for affordable and ethical housing solutions, and even educating yourself on the impacts of homelessness can be a huge step to mold the future of Canada.Â
